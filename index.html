THE ORCA CROWD

Dedicated to the welfare and protection of orcas worldwide.

Our Mission

Our mission is to create a world where all orcas can thrive in their natural, wild environments. Founded in 2007 by Shannon Forsythe, The Orca Crowd is a non-profit organisation dedicated to the welfare and protection of orcas worldwide.

Orcas don't belong in captivity

Orcas are extremely intelligent and social mammals that swim vast distances in the wild – it’s no surprise that stress-related behaviours in captive orcas have been documented for decades! It’s time to end captivity.

Every day in the wild, orcas:

  • SWIM up to 40 miles
  • DIVE 100-500 feet - several times a day

Did you know?

Orcas have the second largest brain of any animal on the planet. And – just like humans - their incredible brains are highly developed in the areas of social intelligence, self-awareness and language.

History

  • Orcas have been kept in captivity since 1961.
  • At least 166 orcas have been taken into captivity from the wild since 1961.
  • 70 orcas have been born in captivity around the world since 1977 – another 30 were stillborn or died in utero.
  • 37 of them are now dead. Only a handful of wild-caught orcas have survived beyond the age of 30. No captive-born orca yet has.

Get to know ...

Corky and Fife

Corky, held at SeaWorld in San Diego, is the longest surviving orca in captivity. She was captured in 1969 from the Northern Resident orca population that that man the waters of Vancouver Island, Canada. She has had 7 offspring in captivity – none have survived.

Corky’s family (known as the A5 pod), including her brother, Fife, continue to thrive in the wild.

Campaigns

Rehabilitation and release

Sadly, captive orcas can’t simply be released in to the wild because they have learned to rely on being fed by humans. We have partnered with The Whale Sanctuary Project , which aims to establish ocean sanctuaries for rescued and retired dolphins and whales.

Your support can help us create these sanctuaries to allow captive orcas to thrive in their natural habitat, and even be re-introduced to the wild.

End captivity

At least 60 orcas are held in captivity around the world - some are wild-caught; some were born in captivity. Since 2002, at least 19 orcas have been taken from the wild into captivity, most recently in Russia.

Your support can help us put pressure on governments around the world to finally ban the breeding of captive orcas and the capture of wild whales.

Stop the demand

Education and raising public awareness plays an essential role in our mission by ending the demand for performing orcas. No demand means no more orcas will need to be exploited for human entertainment – and then there’s no reason for them to kept in small tanks!

Your support can help us continue to fund and develop increased education and schooling programs worldwide.
